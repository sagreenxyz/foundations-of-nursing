## Part 1: Why Break Into Subtasks?

1. **What is the problem with drafting Chapter 2 as a single task?**

   Chapter 2 (*Legal, Ethical, and Professional Standards*) is estimated at 50–60 pages and 11,000–14,000 words across thirteen sections (2.1–2.13), plus a case study, ten NCLEX questions, a summary, and a full reference list. Attempting to produce the entire chapter in a single work session creates:
   - Unacceptably long generation tasks prone to timeout or quality degradation
   - No intermediate checkpoints for review or course correction
   - Inability to parallelize review and drafting

2. **What is the subtask strategy?**

   Chapter 2 is divided into six sequentially deliverable subtasks. Each subtask represents a coherent subset of the chapter that can be drafted, reviewed, and committed on its own.
