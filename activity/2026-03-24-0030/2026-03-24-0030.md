## Q3: What content must Chapter 4 cover?

- Components of a comprehensive health history: chief complaint, HPI (OLDCARTS), PMH, family history, social history, review of systems
- Cultural, spiritual, and psychosocial dimensions of assessment
- Techniques of physical examination: inspection, palpation, percussion, auscultation
- General survey and vital signs (including orthostatic vital signs, pain assessment)
- Head-to-toe systematic physical examination for the adult medical-surgical patient
- Neurological assessment: level of consciousness, GCS, cranial nerves, motor/sensory, NIHSS overview
- Cardiovascular assessment: heart sounds, peripheral vascular, edema grading
- Respiratory assessment: breath sounds, respiratory patterns, oxygen saturation interpretation
- Abdominal assessment: sequence change (inspection, auscultation, percussion, palpation)
- Musculoskeletal and integumentary assessment: skin integrity, Braden Scale, pressure injury staging
- Documentation of assessment findings: SBAR, structured nursing notes, problem-based versus systems-based
