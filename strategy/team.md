Here are the key roles you'd need for a textbook production team:

**Content & Academic**
- **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)** — Deep expertise in the topic; responsible for accuracy, depth, and currency of content. Often the primary authors.
- **Instructional Designer** — Translates expert knowledge into learnable sequences; structures chapters, learning objectives, scaffolding, and assessments.
- **Curriculum Consultant** — Ensures alignment with educational standards (e.g., Common Core, AP, accreditation bodies) for the target grade/level.

**Writing & Editing**
- **Lead Author / Content Writer** — Synthesizes SME input into clear, engaging prose appropriate for the target audience.
- **Developmental Editor** — Works at the macro level: structure, flow, coherence, and pedagogical logic across the whole book.
- **Copy Editor** — Polishes language, grammar, style consistency, and adherence to a style guide (APA, Chicago, etc.).
- **Proofreader** — Final pass for typos, formatting errors, and lingering inconsistencies before print/publication.

**Visual & Design**
- **Graphic Designer / Layout Artist** — Handles typesetting, page layout, and visual hierarchy (typically using InDesign or similar tools).
- **Illustrator / Figure Creator** — Produces diagrams, charts, and original artwork that support the text.
- **Photo Researcher** — Sources and clears rights for photographs and external images.

**Assessment & Pedagogy**
- **Assessment Specialist** — Writes end-of-chapter questions, quizzes, problem sets, and rubrics aligned to learning objectives.
- **Accessibility Specialist** — Ensures content meets standards like WCAG or Section 508 for students with disabilities.

**Technical & Production**
- **Production Manager** — Coordinates timelines, deliverables, and communication across all teams.
- **Digital/eLearning Developer** — If there's a digital companion (LMS integration, interactive content, e-reader formats), this person builds it.
- **Permissions & Rights Manager** — Handles licensing for third-party content (quotes, images, data).

**Review & Quality**
- **Peer Reviewers** — External academics or practitioners who vet the manuscript for accuracy and rigor.
- **Sensitivity Reader** — Reviews for bias, representation, and cultural accuracy.
- **Student/User Tester** — Actual learners from the target audience who test comprehension and usability of draft material.

---

A few practical notes:

- On smaller projects, roles often **overlap** — a good developmental editor may also handle instructional design, or an SME may write their own chapters.
- The **production manager** role is often underestimated but is critical; textbook projects involve dozens of interdependencies.
- If you're targeting **digital-first or open-access** publishing, weight the eLearning developer and accessibility specialist more heavily from the start.
